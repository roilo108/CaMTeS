Overview

The voltage-controlled oscillator (VCO) generates a carrier with constant amplitude and phase in FSK modulation. The VCO's frequency of output depends on the amplitude of the input PNRZ signal. The result of the modulator is an FSK signal. The FSK signal is received in the demodulator and proceeds in two directions. Both paths have a bandpass filter that passes or rejects a signal at a specific frequency, either frequency mark or frequency space. The bandpass filtered signal will undergo an envelope detector. It employs rectification and low pass filtering to detect the envelope of the signal. The comparator subtracts the two input signals from the envelope detectors. Based on the sample, the 1-bit ADC outputs the bitstream.
