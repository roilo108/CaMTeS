CHARACTER CODING

Character coding is also referred to as character set, or charset. Character codes describes a particular encoding of characters in a digital information called bits. It defines how the bits in a character or stream of text are mapped to the character it represent. Character codes represent characters and symbols, such as letters, digits, and punctuation marks. In short, the relationship of bits to character is determined by the character code.

PULSE CODE MODULATION

Pulse Code Modulation is a method that is used to convert an analog signal into a digital signal so that a modified analog signal can be transmitted through the digital communication network.

LINE CODING

Line coding is the process of converting digital data to digital signals. Using this technique a series of bits can be converted to a digital signal. Digital data are encoded into a digital signal at the transmitter side. At the receiver, the digital data is detected from the digital signal by decoding.

DIGITAL MODULATION

Digital modulation is used to transfer a digital bit stream over an analog channel at a high frequency. This enables us to transmit signals generated in a digital circuit across a physical medium. Depending on which parameter of the carrier signal is varied in accordance with the digital message signal, we obtain three main variants of digital modulation called Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK), Frequency Shift Keying (FSK) and Phase Shift Keying (PSK).
